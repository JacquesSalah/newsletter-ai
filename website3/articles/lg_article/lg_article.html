LG Faces Recurring Criticism Over Delivery Delays and Communication Failures With Customers

Consumers report missed deadlines, lack of order updates, and difficulty obtaining clear answers from support

Vitor Perrone May 19 2026

LG Electronics delivery experience has generated dissatisfaction among consumers, according to a series of recent reviews analyzed. Although customers continue to recognize the quality of the brand's products, the reports point to a consistent pattern of logistical delays, poor communication throughout the delivery process, and difficulty obtaining accurate information about orders.

The reviews show that the main problem faced by consumers is related to the failure to meet the deadlines promised at the time of purchase. In several reports, customers state that orders remained “stuck” for several days without updates, while others mention that delivery deadlines were changed more than once throughout the process. The recurrence of this type of situation suggests a possible operational bottleneck in logistics management or in communication between delivery systems and consumers.

Another recurring issue is the sense of uncertainty caused by the lack of clear information regarding order status. Many consumers report that they had to contact support multiple times to understand the reasons for the delays, without receiving concrete estimates. In one of the analyzed reports, a customer states that “support cannot provide a clear estimate,” while another describes the communication as “confusing” throughout the entire delivery process.

Despite the criticism, the reviews also reveal that perceptions regarding the quality of LG’s products remain positive. Some consumers highlight that the items arrived in good condition and reinforce that they still like the brand. However, the praise ends up being overshadowed by frustration caused by delivery delays, especially for higher-value purchases such as televisions and home appliances.

The analysis of the comments suggests that there is a misalignment between the expectations created at the moment of purchase and the experience actually delivered to consumers. When the deadline initially provided is not met and customers do not receive transparent updates, the impact goes beyond the delay itself, directly affecting trust in the brand and perceptions of service reliability.

Consumer experience specialists point out that recurring logistics and communication issues tend to have a significant impact on companies’ digital reputation, especially in a scenario where online reviews directly influence new purchasing decisions. In cases like this, the recurrence of complaints may indicate the need for operational revisions in delivery workflows and customer support processes.

If the pattern identified in the reviews persists, LG may face growing deterioration in its after-sales experience, even while maintaining a good reputation regarding product quality. The combination of frequent delays and insufficient communication tends to increase consumers’ sense of uncertainty, affecting both customer loyalty and the confidence of future buyers.

Main themes identified

  • Recurring delivery delays;
  • Failure to meet the deadlines informed at the time of purchase;
  • Lack of updates regarding order status;
  • Communication perceived as confusing or insufficient;
  • Difficulty obtaining clear answers from support;
  • Sense of uncertainty throughout the delivery process;
  • Product quality still well evaluated by consumers;
  • Frustration caused by the gap between expectations and the actual experience.
